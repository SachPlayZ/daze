# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary users are mainstream World Cup fans building and following a single-match fantasy XI on mobile. They need real lineups, fast team creation, understandable live point impacts, and a trustworthy path from entry through final prize claim. Solana users are welcome, but the experience must not require protocol fluency. Telegram is an optional personal companion for reminders and score changes, not a gameplay client.

## Product Purpose

Daze is the complete fantasy contest for TxLINE-covered World Cup fixtures: connect a Solana wallet, build or Quick Pick a valid XI from verified lineups, lock and enter, follow explainable live scoring and rank movement, then reconcile and claim on devnet. Success means every verified match action produces an immediate, reproducible consequence for the fan without fabricated data or manual intervention.

## Positioning

Build your eleven, then see exactly what every verified match moment did to your points and rank.

## Brand Personality

Expressive, warm, and competitive. Daze feels like polished editorial football culture around the match and sharp live utility during it. Its voice is direct, energetic, transparent, and football-first; blockchain remains quiet infrastructure.

## Anti-references

Daze must not resemble a crypto casino, betting terminal, degen or cyberpunk dashboard, generic Web3 landing page, esports skin, or developer console. Avoid purple-on-black defaults, token tickers, casino motifs, excessive glassmorphism, neon glows, dense gameplay content on gradients, unlicensed football imagery, and protocol-first consumer copy.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the fan's task and football language; reveal chain details progressively.
2. Keep expressive brand moments separate from crisp, legible gameplay surfaces.
3. Reserve high contrast and motion for genuine live state, points, rank, captaincy, and urgent actions.
4. Explain every scoring consequence and distinguish provisional, corrected, and final truth.
5. Make trust visible through verified lineups, feed health, TxLINE provenance, and deterministic reconciliation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA: at least 4.5:1 contrast for body text and 3:1 for large text and controls. Never rely on color alone. Provide visible focus, 44×44px touch targets, non-disruptive live announcements, reduced-motion behavior, a complete 320px-wide core flow without horizontal scrolling, and support for 200% text scaling. Positive, negative, live, provisional, final, and correction states need explicit text or icon cues.
